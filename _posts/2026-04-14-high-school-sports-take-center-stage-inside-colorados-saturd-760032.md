## A Weekend Hub for Colorado High School Athletics

For student-athletes and sports fans across Colorado, the weekend serves as more than just a break from the classroom; it is a time to celebrate the hard work and achievements of the state's high school teams. At the center of this celebration is the "Saturday Prep Rally" on 9NEWS, a program dedicated to capturing the intensity and community spirit of local sports. Based in Denver, the station provides a comprehensive weekly roundup that ensures no major moment from the Colorado high school athletic scene goes unnoticed.

The program serves as a vital platform for showcasing high school sports, offering viewers a dedicated space to catch up on the latest news, scores, and video highlights. While many news outlets prioritize professional leagues, the "Prep Rally" places the spotlight firmly on the younger generation of competitors, acknowledging the significance of high school sports within the local culture. According to 9news.com, the show provides an opportunity for the community to "catch up on the latest high school sports news" in a consolidated, high-energy format.

## Engaging the Community and the Honor Roll

The "Prep Rally" is not merely a one-way broadcast; it is structured to foster community engagement. One of the program’s standout features is the "9NEWS Prep Rally Honor Roll." This interactive element allows viewers to participate directly by voting for their favorite high school sports moments of the week. By involving the audience in the selection process, the station turns the broadcast into a shared community experience, where fans can rally behind their local heroes.

In addition to the interactive Honor Roll, 9NEWS maintains a High School Scoreboard on its website, ensuring that fans have access to the latest data even outside of the broadcast windows. This commitment to data-driven reporting, combined with the visual storytelling of extended game highlights, creates a multi-layered approach to sports journalism. The station actively encourages this local connection by inviting viewers to submit story ideas. Whether through email at sports@9news.com or social media platforms like X, the sports department remains open to community-sourced leads, allowing the narrative of Colorado sports to be shaped by those who live it. Viewers are encouraged to reach out to Ashley Moore directly or use handles such as @9Preps and @9sportsco to share noteworthy achievements.

## The Faces Behind the Coverage

The success of the "Prep Rally" is driven by a team of dedicated reporters who bring professional sports journalism to the high school level. Ashley Moore, a 9NEWS Prep Sports Reporter, is a frequent presence on the program, often serving as the host. Her reporting provides the expertise and local knowledge necessary to cover a wide variety of sports across a diverse state. According to 9news.com, viewers are encouraged to check back for her coverage "every Saturday and Sunday" to see the latest highlights.

Joining Moore in the coverage is John Tish, who has also been featured as a host of the program. Tish’s approach to the broadcast highlights the enthusiasm that the station brings to high school sports. During a broadcast featured on YouTube, Tish introduced the show by saying, "We welcome to the 9 News prep rally. Good morning, what an opportunity to celebrate high school sports. This is the Prep Rally. I'm John Tish coming off the bench with enthusiasm." This tone reflects the station’s broader goal: to treat high school athletics with the same seriousness and excitement as professional sports.

## Scheduling and Ongoing Presence

The "Prep Rally" has established a consistent presence in the Denver media landscape. The program airs on both Saturday and Sunday mornings, providing multiple opportunities for viewers to engage with the content. While specific details regarding the show’s original start date are not explicitly detailed in recent station reports, the sheer volume of recent entries on 9news.com for both weekend time slots indicates that the program is a regular and ongoing fixture in the station’s programming lineup.

The production and its extensive coverage are powered by Excel Energy, highlighting the role of corporate support in maintaining local community programming. This partnership allows 9NEWS to maintain a high standard of production, including the collection and airing of high-quality video highlights from games that might otherwise receive little media attention. By providing in-depth coverage of individual athlete achievements, the station helps foster a sense of pride within schools and neighborhoods across Colorado.

While the "Prep Rally" serves as a celebratory platform for student-athletes, the structured focus on highlights and "honor rolls" raises questions about what falls outside the frame. The reliance on community submissions and social media engagement suggests a democratic approach to news gathering, but it also means that schools with more active social media presences or more vocal booster clubs may receive disproportionate coverage. By framing the news through the lens of "celebration" and "enthusiasm," the program risks prioritizing the feel-good narrative of victory and individual achievement over the more complex challenges facing high school athletics, such as funding disparities between districts or the physical toll of high-stakes youth competition. As high school sports coverage becomes increasingly polished and sponsored, the line between community journalism and promotional content continues to blur, leaving one to wonder if the "rally" is more about the sports themselves or the brand that powers them.
